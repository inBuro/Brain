# Product — Tone of Voice

**Source of truth**: "Performance Flow" section of the landing page (user-supplied 2026-05-20).
All rules below are extracted from that text, not invented.

---

## Voice characteristics

**1. Mechanic-first, benefit-implied**
Describe what the control does, in concrete terms. Let the benefit surface on its own. Never announce the benefit before showing the mechanism.

**2. Numerical specificity over relative praise**
Numbers replace adjectives. If something is "more," say how much more. If something changes, name the before and after.

**3. Short sentences with a fixed two-beat rhythm**
First sentence: the mechanism or action. Second sentence: the consequence or payoff. Period. Move on.

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Modes 1–10 control instruments; 11–14 become a dedicated mixer." | "Effortlessly organize your instruments and mixer into logical groups." |
| "One hotkey jumps between instruments and mixer." | "Seamlessly switch between your instruments and mixer with a single keystroke." |
| "6 controls instead of 3." | "Double your encoder coverage for maximum flexibility." |
| "Hold a button to preview the next page. Turn the mode encoder to stay there." | "Easily preview or lock into any page — it's that simple." |
| "Every mode change can be mapped to a keyboard shortcut." | "Fully mappable shortcuts give you complete hands-free control." |
| "Switch modes without reaching for hardware." | "Stay in the flow without interrupting your creative process." |

---

## Anti-markers (never use)

- Vague amplifiers: *seamless, powerful, flexible, effortless, complete, ultimate, advanced*
- Filler benefit phrases: *stay in the flow, take your performance to the next level, the way music should be made*
- Passive voice in feature descriptions
- Exclamation marks
- Emoji in UI or landing copy
- Rhetorical questions in feature blocks (FAQ is the one exception)

---

## Structural rules derived from the reference

- **Feature headline = verb phrase**: "Move between instruments and mixer instantly", "Run the whole rig from the keyboard" — never a noun phrase like "Instant switching" or "Full keyboard control".
- **Feature body = exactly two sentences**: sentence 1 states the mechanism; sentence 2 states the result or the second half of the mechanism. No third sentence.
- **Numbers always explicit**: write "6 encoders", "Modes 1–10", "6 controls instead of 3" — never "multiple", "several", "more encoders".
- **Subject of action = the control or the user, never the product**: "One hotkey jumps", "Hold a button", "Turn the encoder" — not "XL Performance lets you jump" or "Our system enables…"

---

## CTA rules (from existing approved copy + reference pattern)

- Verb-first: "Buy on Gumroad • $39", "Watch the demo", "See it in your workflow"
- Price inline in primary CTA when present: "Buy on Gumroad • $39" not "Buy now"
- Short form for secondary: "Watch the demo", "Subscribe" — no filler

---

## Product name in copy

- Full: `Fadercraft XL Performance` — first mention per page/section
- Short: `XL Performance` — subsequent mentions in body
- Hardware: always `Launch Control XL MK3` (not "LCXL" in customer-facing copy, "LCXL" acceptable in eyebrows and tight UI labels)
- Device file: `XL_Performance.amxd` — monospace / code style when referencing the file directly
