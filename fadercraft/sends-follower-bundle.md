## Quickstart intro (draft)

Sends Follower sits on a return track. It reads how hard every track is sending to that
return, takes the loudest, and feeds that into the LFO sitting right after it in the rack.
Map that LFO onto the effects on your return — up to eight parameters, each with its own Min
and Max — and they move with your mix. Drop the rack on a return, map the LFO to a reverb or
filter, and play.

## Effects to drop on a return (all stock Ableton — nothing to buy)

*What to modulate with the LFO in italics.*

- **Reverb** — Reverb, Hybrid Reverb — *Decay / Size, Dry/Wet, Diffusion*
- **Delay / Echo** — Delay, Echo, Filter Delay, Grain Delay — *Feedback, Time, Filter freq, Dry/Wet*
- **Filter** — Auto Filter — *Cutoff, Resonance*
- **Modulation** — Chorus-Ensemble, Phaser-Flanger — *Amount/Depth, Rate, Feedback*
- **Saturation / Drive** — Roar, Saturator, Overdrive, Dynamic Tube — *Drive/Amount, Tone/Color*
- **Pitch / Spectral / weird** — Frequency Shifter, Shifter, Spectral Resonator, Spectral Time, Corpus, Resonators — *Frequency/Pitch, Decay, Blend*
- **Lo-fi / texture** — Erosion, Redux, Vinyl Distortion, Beat Repeat — *Amount, Grid/Chance*

Third-party effects work the same — map the LFO to any parameter.
